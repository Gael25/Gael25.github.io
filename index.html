About

I hold a Master's degree in Development Economics from Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne University. Since 2024, I have been working as a research assistant on several applied economics projects at the Geneva Graduate Institute, the London Business School, the University of Namur, and Paris 1.

My work focuses on building and analyzing datasets from heterogeneous sources, including administrative records, survey data, spatial data, and historical archives, primarily in African and French contexts. I have experience with R, Stata, Python, QGIS, and LaTeX.

My research interests include political economy, development economics, and public policy evaluation. I am particularly interested in questions related to state capacity, governance, and the empirical study of institutions.
